# CHESS Summer Student Onboarding
### Welcome to the CHESS Summer Program for Quantum Materials Beamline (QM2)! This guide outlines the required steps and resources to help ensure a smooth onboarding process for all students!

#### ✅ Account Setup
CLASSE IDs and BeamPASS logins are created by the IT group before students arrive. Make sure you can log in to both systems prior to your first day.

#### 📅 CHESS User Meeting

All students are expected to attend the CHESS User Meeting. This meeting provides important information about facility usage and expectations.

BeamPASS – Instructions for Wilson Lab Students

Students at Wilson Lab will complete the CHESS User training and quizzes through BeamPASS to be assigned monthly radiation badges. Specific instructions for accessing BeamPASS and completing the training are as follows:
Create a CLASSE ID (if you don’t have one):
Visit the BeamPASS login page.
Click on “Don’t have a CLASSE ID?” and complete the form with your name and email.
You’ll receive a confirmation email. Once we process your request, you’ll receive a second email containing your assigned CLASSE ID and a link to reset your password.
Important: Complete this step promptly, as it may require manual input from CHESS.
Log In to BeamPASS:
Access BeamPASS using your CLASSE ID and password.
Please complete or update your personal record.
If you encounter any login issues, please contact service-classe@cornell.edu and chessuseroffice@cornell.edu.
Training (Please make sure to review all of the provided material and complete both exams):
Log into BeamPASS using the assigned CLASSE ID and the password you set.
Review the User Orientation & Safety Training and the User Guide.
Complete the following exams:
i.      In-Person User Exam: An online exam for all CHESS experimenters.
ii.      CHESS User Exam: A short online exam for users collecting data at the beamline.

Training

Workday Learning (WDL) is where the majority of relevant safety trainings will be completed. As external learners in Workday, you should have received emails from Workday with unique login credentials to use (separate from Cornell or CLASSE logins) along with instructions for accessing Workday.
All students will be required to complete “CU 601 – Responsible and Ethical Conduct of Research and Research Safety” (AKA RCR Training) via Workday Learning. Complete this training by 6/12 and send a record (e.g., screenshot of completion) to Katerina Malysheva (evm23@cornell.edu).  
If there are issues related to logging into Workday Learning, please reach out to Workday support (workdaylearning@cornell.edu)
